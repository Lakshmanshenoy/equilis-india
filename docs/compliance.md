# Compliance Reference

## Legal Context

### SEBI Research Analyst Regulations 2014 (RA Regulations)
The SEBI (Research Analysts) Regulations 2014 govern the publication of research reports
and investment advice in India. Key provisions relevant to this system:

**Regulation 2(1)(w) — Research Report:**
> "Research report means any written or electronic communication that includes analysis
> of securities or a sector with the purpose of making an investment recommendation."

**Equilis India is NOT a SEBI-registered research analyst.** This system produces
data analysis and scenario constructs for educational and informational purposes only.
It does not constitute a research report under RA Regulations.

### SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/IMD/IMD-I/DOF5/P/CIR/2022 (AI/ML in Finance)
SEBI has issued guidance on the use of AI/ML tools in financial analysis. All AI-generated
outputs must clearly disclose their AI/ML origin and must not be presented as registered
research or investment advice.

## Prohibited Language

The following words and phrases MUST NOT appear in any output from this system:

**Explicit recommendations:**
- buy, sell, hold, accumulate, reduce
- outperform, underperform, market perform, neutral, overweight, underweight
- strong buy, strong sell

**Price target language:**
- price target, fair value, intrinsic value, target price
- "worth ₹X", "should be trading at", "expected to reach ₹X"
- "upside of X%", "downside of X%"

**Advice-like language:**
- "you should invest", "investors should", "we recommend"
- "a good opportunity", "investors can consider"

**Safe alternatives:**
| Prohibited phrase         | Use instead                                  |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| "Price target: ₹1,500"   | "At PE 20× on Base scenario: ₹1,482"        |
| "Buy — 25% upside"        | "Base scenario implies ₹X at PE 20×"         |
| "Hold for long term"      | "3-year scenario range: ₹X–₹Y"              |
| "Outperform peers"        | "Higher ROE/ROCE than sector median"          |
| "Fair value: ₹X"          | "Scenario analysis range: ₹X–₹Y"            |

## Model Disclaimers (Required)

### Altman Z-Score
> Altman Z-Score is an academic model (Altman, 1968) calibrated on publicly traded
> US manufacturing firms. It must not be interpreted as a credit rating or insolvency
> prediction for Indian companies, financial entities, or service-sector firms. Use
> as one of many data points in a broader analysis.

### Beneish M-Score
> Beneish M-Score (Beneish, 1999) is a probabilistic earnings-quality screen calibrated
> on US companies. A score above −1.78 indicates statistical similarity to manipulator
> profiles in the training sample — it is not an allegation of fraud or manipulation.
> Always corroborate with auditor notes, related-party disclosures, and cash flow analysis.

### Scenario Analysis
> Scenarios are based on stated growth and multiple assumptions. They are not
> analyst forecasts, earnings estimates, or price targets. The range of scenario
> outputs does not bound actual outcomes.

## Data Attribution Requirements

Every financial figure in a report must be attributed with:
1. The data source (e.g., Screener.in, NSE API, BSE Filings)
2. The date the data was fetched (ISO 8601 format)
3. The field name as it appears in the source

Example:
> Revenue TTM: ₹153,670 Cr (Source: Screener.in; fetched 2024-03-31)

This requirement is enforced by `core/validator.py` and `core/renderer.py`.
