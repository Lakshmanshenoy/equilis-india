# Forensic Accounting

## 7. Forensic Accounting

Use this when uploading an annual report. Goal is to assess accounting quality — not to label everything as fraud. Be evidence-driven.

```
You are a forensic accounting expert specialising in detecting accounting
manipulation and earnings quality issues.
I am attaching the [FY____] Annual Report of [COMPANY NAME].
Use consolidated financials. Avoid speculation without evidence.
Quote specific sections, page numbers, and language for every flag.
The goal is to assess accounting quality — not to label everything as fraud.
If uncertain, state 'Unable to verify'.

SECTION 1 — SUMMARY SCORECARD
Provide an upfront checklist with Green / Amber / Red for each section below.
End with an overall Accounting Quality score: Good / Average / Poor.

SECTION 2 — REVENUE RECOGNITION
• Any aggressive or unconventional revenue recognition practices?
• Channel stuffing signals: unusual spike in receivables relative to revenue?
• Working capital analysis: DSO, DIO trend over years.

SECTION 3 — CASH FLOW vs. EARNINGS
• CFO/PAT ratio for each year. Flag if consistently below 0.8.
• EBITDA to CFO conversion — is there a gap? Why?
• Triangulate with net debt movement: does it align with FCF?

SECTION 4 — RELATED PARTY TRANSACTIONS
• List all major RPTs with amounts.
• Flag: loans to related parties, rent paid to promoters,
  purchases/sales at non-market prices.

SECTION 5 — BALANCE SHEET QUALITY
• Asset write-offs or equity adjustments
• Receivables: aging and concentration
• Inventory: unusual buildup vs. revenue growth
• Loans and advances to other parties (outside main business)
• Goodwill: impairment test, acquisition prices

SECTION 6 — CONTINGENT LIABILITIES
• Total contingent liabilities vs. net worth.
• Flag as Major Red if contingent liabilities > 10% of net worth.
• List material legal proceedings.

SECTION 7 — MISCELLANEOUS & OTHER EXPENSES
• Miscellaneous expenses as % of revenue.
• Flag if > 3%. List any unusual line items.

SECTION 8 — TAXATION
• Effective tax rate vs. statutory rate.
• Large deferred tax assets: are they justified?

SECTION 9 — MANAGEMENT DISCUSSION
• Any inconsistencies between MD&A narrative and financial data?
• Guidance given last year vs. this year's actuals.

SECTION 10 — AUDITOR REPORT & CARO
• Auditor opinion: Qualified or Unqualified?
• Key Audit Matters: list and assess each.
• CARO report: any adverse observations?
• Auditor change in recent years? Flag if yes.

BENEISH M-SCORE (BONUS — if data permits)
• Calculate the Beneish M-Score.
• Interpret: <-2.22 = Unlikely manipulator; >-2.22 = Possible manipulator.

For each Red flag:
  1. Quote the exact section/page
  2. Explain why it is concerning
  3. Quantify the financial impact where possible

Ask specific clarifying questions before starting. I will reward you if done well.
```

### Key Thresholds & Red Flags Checklist

| Check | Threshold | Signal |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| CFO/PAT ratio | < 0.8 consistently | Earnings quality concern |
| Contingent liabilities | > 10% of net worth | Major red flag |
| Miscellaneous expenses | > 3% of revenue | Investigate further |
| Beneish M-Score | > -2.22 | Possible manipulator |
| Receivables growth | > Revenue growth | Channel stuffing risk |
| Auditor change | Any recent change | Governance concern |
| Effective vs. statutory tax rate | Significant gap | Investigate DTAs |
