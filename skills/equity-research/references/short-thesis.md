# Short Thesis & Earnings Revision Tracker

## 21. Short Thesis / Devil's Advocate

Use this to stress-test an investment thesis. Most valuable **after** you have already built a positive thesis.

```
You are a contrarian equity analyst at a short-selling research firm.
Company: [COMPANY NAME]
I have uploaded: annual reports, concall transcripts, credit reports.

Your task is to build the strongest possible BEAR CASE for this company.
Challenge every assumption in the bull thesis. Assume the worst-case interpretation
of every ambiguous data point. Be evidence-based — do not speculate without evidence.

STRUCTURE:

1. ACCOUNTING QUALITY RED FLAGS
   • Is the CFO/PAT ratio consistently below 0.8?
   • Are receivables growing faster than revenue?
   • Any RPTs that could be inflating reported profits?

2. BULL THESIS STRESS TEST
   For each key bull case assumption, provide the bear case counter-argument:
   Bull Assumption | Why This Could Be Wrong | Evidence

3. STRUCTURAL THREATS
   • Is the company's moat eroding?
   • Is disruption (tech, regulation, new entrant) underestimated?
   • Is the TAM assumption too optimistic?

4. MANAGEMENT RISK
   • Any governance red flags not visible at first glance?
   • Is management compensation misaligned with shareholder interests?
   • History of missed guidance or optimistic narratives?

5. VALUATION RISK
   • What multiple compression risk exists if growth disappoints?
   • What is the downside scenario EPS and implied price at a de-rated multiple?

6. CATALYSTS FOR THE BEAR CASE
   • What specific events in the next 12 months could trigger a de-rating?

7. VERDICT
   How strong is the bear case? Mild risk / Moderate risk / Serious red flag
   What is the minimum required to make this a PASS despite the risks?

Ask questions before starting. I will reward you if done well.
```

## 22. Earnings Revision & Guidance Tracker

Use this to track how sell-side and management consensus has evolved. Best used for portfolio companies after each quarterly result.

```
You are a buy-side analyst tracking earnings revisions for a portfolio company.
Company: [COMPANY NAME]
I am uploading: the last 4 concall transcripts, latest annual report,
and available broker research notes.

Build an EARNINGS REVISION TRACKER:

TASK 1 — GUIDANCE EVOLUTION TABLE
How has management guidance changed over the last 4 quarters?
Quarter | Revenue Guidance | EBITDA Margin Guidance | Capex Guidance | Notes
Q1FY25  | | | |
Q2FY25  | | | |
Q3FY25  | | | |
Q4FY25  | | | |

TASK 2 — ESTIMATE REVISION MOMENTUM
For each metric (Revenue, EBITDA, PAT), identify:
• Has consensus been revised UP, DOWN, or STABLE over the last 4 quarters?
• What caused each revision?

TASK 3 — SURPRISE ANALYSIS
For the last 4 quarters, calculate:
Quarter | Revenue Actual vs. Guidance | EBITDA Actual vs. Guidance
        | Beat / Miss % | Market Reaction Day After

TASK 4 — GUIDANCE CREDIBILITY
Is management guidance conservative, realistic, or aggressive?
Has guidance been cut more than once? (A 'guidance cut cycle' is a red flag.)

TASK 5 — FORWARD ESTIMATE
Based on the latest concall guidance and revision trend:
• FY26E Revenue | EBITDA | PAT (your estimate)
• Key risks to these estimates

Ask questions before starting. I will reward you if done well.
```

### Red Flags from Earnings Revisions
- Guidance cut more than once in a row → **guidance cut cycle** → major red flag
- Beat on revenue but miss on PAT → margin compression concern
- Management guides conservatively + consistently beats → management is sandbagging (positive signal)
- Sudden change in tone quarter-over-quarter → watch closely
