# Moat Analysis & Management Quality

## 19. Moat Analysis

Use this to systematically evaluate the durability and depth of a company's competitive advantage. Works best with 5–10 years of financial data.

```
You are an expert equity analyst specialising in competitive advantage analysis.
Company: [COMPANY NAME] | Sector: [SECTOR]
Data: uploaded annual reports + screener.in link + any additional sources

Analyse the company's economic moat using the following frameworks:

PART A — MOAT TYPE IDENTIFICATION
Assess each potential moat source (Strong / Narrow / Absent + evidence):
1. Cost Advantage: lower production/distribution costs than competitors?
2. Network Effect: does value increase as more users join?
3. Switching Costs: how painful/costly is it for a customer to leave?
4. Intangible Assets: brand, patents, regulatory licences, proprietary data
5. Efficient Scale: does market structure limit competition?
6. Distribution Advantage: unique channel or reach?

PART B — MOAT DURABILITY TEST
1. Has ROCE consistently exceeded cost of capital (10%) over 5–7 years?
   [Weak moat = ROCE occasionally > 10%; Strong moat = consistently > 15%]
2. Has gross margin been stable or expanding over 7 years?
3. Has the company maintained/grown market share against new entrants?
4. Are there any structural threats to the moat?
   (tech disruption, regulation, commodity exposure, PE-backed competition)

PART C — PORTER'S FIVE FORCES (brief)
Rate each force: High / Medium / Low threat
• Competitive Rivalry
• Threat of New Entrants
• Threat of Substitutes
• Buyer Power
• Supplier Power

PART D — MOAT VERDICT
Wide Moat / Narrow Moat / No Moat — with a 3-line justification.
Is the moat widening, stable, or eroding?
What is the single biggest threat to the moat in the next 5 years?

Ask questions before starting. I will reward you if done well.
```

### Moat Durability Signals

| Signal | Strong Moat | Narrow Moat | No Moat |
|--------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| ROCE | Consistently > 15% | Occasionally > 10% | < 10% |
| Gross margin trend | Stable/expanding over 7Y | Stable | Eroding |
| Market share | Gaining | Stable | Losing |

## 18. Management Quality & Capital Allocation

A dedicated prompt to assess management quality. Use annual reports, concalls, and public filings.

```
You are an expert buy-side analyst focused on management quality assessment.
Company: [COMPANY NAME]
I am attaching: annual reports (5 years), concall transcripts, credit reports.

PART A — MANAGEMENT QUALITY SCORECARD

1. PROMOTER BACKGROUND & TRACK RECORD
   • Business history and promoter pedigree
   • Any past business failures, controversies, or litigations
   • How did management navigate the last major business cycle/stress?

2. CAPITAL ALLOCATION TRACK RECORD (last 5 years)
   Categorise all major capital deployment decisions:
   Decision | Amount (₹ Cr) | Outcome | ROCE Generated | Good/Bad Decision
   Includes: capex, acquisitions, dividend, buybacks, fund raises

3. COMPENSATION STRUCTURE
   • Management remuneration as % of revenue and % of PAT
   • Is it performance-linked (linked to PAT/ROCE/EVA)?
   • Trend: did compensation rise even when profits fell?

4. COMMUNICATION QUALITY
   • Are concall answers direct and specific, or evasive and vague?
   • Do they acknowledge when they miss guidance?
   • Do they volunteer bad news, or bury it?

5. PROMOTER BEHAVIOUR
   • Promoter shareholding trend (increasing / stable / decreasing)
   • Any promoter pledging? Current level and trend.
   • Insider buying/selling in past 12 months

6. GOVERNANCE FLAGS
   • RPT intensity vs. peers
   • Auditor changes in last 5 years
   • Any SEBI/NCLT/court orders against the company or promoters
   • Board independence: % of independent directors, their background

PART B — OVERALL VERDICT
Rate management on each dimension: 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent)
Dimension | Score | Key Evidence
Capital Allocation | |
Operational Execution | |
Communication Quality | |
Governance Standards | |
Compensation Fairness | |
Overall Management Score: ___ / 25

Ask questions before starting. I will reward you if done well.
```
