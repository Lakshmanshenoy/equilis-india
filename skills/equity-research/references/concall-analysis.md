# Conference Call Analysis

## 4. Conference Call — Deep Analysis

Use this when you upload a full concall transcript. Optimised for portfolio companies where you need institutional-grade depth.

```
You are my senior buy-side analyst. I am a fund manager.
[COMPANY NAME] is a portfolio company. I am uploading the [Q__FY__] concall transcript.
Be factual and comprehensive. Avoid unsupported inferences.
For industry- and company-specific topics, provide deep explanation — do not summarise.
If uncertain about any fact, state 'Unable to verify'.

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
   • Overall performance vs. expectations
   • Key topics discussed
   • Management tone: confident / cautious / defensive / contradictory
   • Any notable shift in tone vs. last call?

2. DETAILED ANALYSIS
   • Business Model Evolution: revenue impact, cost structure, working capital changes
   • Industry Environment: cycles, regulation, disruption
   • Key Business Insights: critical insights on business and environment
   • KPI Trends: expectations, actuals, and drivers
   • Capital Allocation: plans and rationale for capex, M&A, buybacks

3. INDUSTRY & COMPANY SPECIFICS
   Provide deep explanation (not summary) of:
   • Industry: market trends, competitive landscape, regulatory/policy changes,
     supply chain dynamics, geopolitical factors
   • Company: operational challenges, strategic priorities, customer demand,
     order flow, client-specific updates
   • Management's thought process in navigating each topic
   • Flag any insight you found particularly interesting or non-obvious

4. FORWARD-LOOKING GUIDANCE
   • Revenue guidance (quantitative where available)
   • Margin outlook: gross, EBITDA, PAT
   • Growth drivers: organic, geographic, new products
   • Recovery commentary: timeline, confidence level, contributing factors
   • List ALL quantitative guidance in a dedicated table

5. WALK-THE-TALK CHECK
   • Pull guidance given in the PREVIOUS call (from transcript context if available)
   • Compare to actuals reported this call
   • Rate each guidance item: ✅ Met / 🟡 Partially Met / ❌ Missed

6. RISK ASSESSMENT
   • Competitive, regulatory, technology, execution, and market-specific risks
   • Support each risk with evidence from the transcript

7. PEER COMPARISON
   • Any mention of peer performance or competitive dynamics

8. ANALYST Q&A
   • For each key question: analyst name/firm, question, full management response
   • Recurring concerns across multiple analysts
   • Dodged or incomplete answers — flag and speculate on reasons
   • Suggested follow-up questions for gaps

9. QUANTITATIVE DATA TABLE
   Table: Metric | Value | Period | Source (page/section in transcript)

10. KEY INSIGHTS TABLE
    Table: Insight | Positive/Negative | Evidence

11. CONNECTING THE DOTS
    Integrate management messaging with financial and strategic implications.

12. ANALYSTS ON CALL
    List all analyst names and firms who asked questions.

13. CONVICTION SCORE
    Rate management credibility on this call: 1–10, with reasoning.

Ask specific questions before starting. I will reward you if done well.
```

## 5. Conference Call — Brief Analysis

Use this for a rapid scan — e.g., for sector monitoring or initial screening.

```
You are a financial research assistant analysing a concall transcript.
Use only the content of the transcript. Do not use external data or assumptions.
Keep each section to 3–5 bullet points. Be direct and dense with information.
If uncertain, state 'Unable to verify'. I have attached the transcript.

1. MANAGEMENT COMMENTARY
   • Key statements from CEO and CFO
   • Tone: Positive / Neutral / Cautious / Defensive
   • Areas of emphasis or avoidance

2. GUIDANCE & FORWARD OUTLOOK
   • Revenue, margin, and volume guidance
   • Demand visibility vs. uncertainty
   • Qualitative or quantitative projections

3. INDUSTRY & MACRO
   • Industry growth, cyclicality, regulatory commentary
   • Broader economic observations (inflation, input costs, demand)

4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
   • Performance vs. peers (if mentioned)
   • Pricing power, differentiation, strategic positioning

5. RISKS & CONCERNS
   • RM cost, forex, regulatory, customer churn risks
   • Any cautious or repeated management statements

6. GROWTH DRIVERS & INITIATIVES
   • New products, geographies, partnerships
   • Capex plans, cost efficiency, M&A

7. PRODUCT MIX & PORTFOLIO
   • Volume/value shift, premiumisation, new launches

8. FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS (if disclosed)
   • Revenue, EBITDA, PAT, margins, debt, cash flow (brief only)

9. SENTIMENT ANALYSIS
   • Overall tone: Positive / Neutral / Negative
   • Confidence level: High / Moderate / Low
   • Notable shift from prior call language (if discernible)

CONCLUSION: 3-sentence summary of the single most important takeaway.

If uncertain, state 'Unable to verify'. I will reward you if done well.
```
