# Thesis Building: Fundamental Checklist, Growth Triggers & Walk the Talk

## 10. Growth Triggers Checklist (One-Pager)

Use when researching a potential investment. Upload annual reports, concalls, credit ratings, and equity reports.

```
You are an expert equity analyst at a world-class equity research firm,
specialising in creating forward-looking growth trigger checklists.

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
I am attaching: annual reports, concall transcripts, credit rating reports,
equity research notes. You may also use external sources.

Data Sources:
• Company annual reports and concall transcripts (attached)
• Screener.in: [paste link]
• Credit rating reports (CRISIL/ICRA) (attached)
• Broker research (attached or from t.me/researchreportss)

CHECKLIST (Yes / Partial / No — with 2-line reasoning for each):

1. GROWING TAM
   Is the industry's total addressable market expanding?
   Quantify the TAM and company's current market share.

2. OPERATING LEVERAGE
   Is there scope for fixed cost dilution as revenues scale?
   What is the incremental EBITDA margin on new revenue?

3. VALUE CHAIN CLIMB
   Is the company moving up the value chain (forward integration,
   higher-value products, IP development)?

4. CAPEX-LED GROWTH
   Describe current capex plans (brownfield / greenfield / M&A).
   When is capacity expected to come online? Revenue visibility?

5. GEOGRAPHIC EXPANSION
   Is the company entering new geographies (domestic Tier 2/3 or export)?

6. NEW PRODUCTS / SEGMENTS
   Any new product launches or business segments?
   Timeline and revenue potential.

7. DELEVERAGING
   Is significant debt being repaid? (Mark Yes only if substantial.)
   Quantify the interest cost saving.

8. CORPORATE ACTIONS
   Any demergers, fund raises, warrants, stake sales, M&A, or strategic
   investments in the pipeline?

9. MANAGEMENT CHANGE
   New management driving operational or strategic transformation?

10. ORDER BOOK / PIPELINE
    (Only for order-book-driven businesses)
    Is the order book expanding? Provide 3-year book-to-bill ratio trend.

PROBABILITY SCORING
For each 'Yes' trigger, assign:
  • Probability of playing out: High / Medium / Low
  • Timeline: <1 year / 1–2 years / 2–3 years

CONCLUSION
• Top 3 growth triggers to track
• Key metrics / quarterly milestones to monitor
• Overall likelihood of growth thesis playing out: High / Medium / Low

Make your answer forward-looking. Use data beyond what I've provided.
I will reward you if done well.
```

## 11. Fundamental Checklist & Thesis Building

Use for a full one-pager investment report on any company. Best used with Q4FY25/TTM data.

```
You are a fundamental equity research analyst preparing a professional-grade,
forward-looking investment report on [COMPANY NAME].

Use only the latest available data (Q4FY25 or TTM).
Avoid outdated metrics. Focus on what drives value over the next 2–3 years.
State 'Unable to verify' if any fact is uncertain.

Data Sources:
• Q4FY25 earnings call transcript and investor presentation
• Screener.in: [paste link]
• Broker research from t.me/researchreportss
• Credit rating reports (CRISIL/ICRA)
• Annual report and investor day decks

STRUCTURE:

1. COMPANY SNAPSHOT
   • Core business description
   • Revenue by segment, geography, product line
   • B2B / B2C / Hybrid classification
   • Key customers, manufacturing, distribution
   • Promoter group and ownership structure

2. BUSINESS MODEL & UNIT ECONOMICS
   Use industry-specific KPIs:
   • Cement: realisation/tonne, cost/tonne
   • Hospitals: ARPOB, occupancy rate
   • Chemicals: contribution margin/kg, capacity utilisation
   • AMC: AUM mix, yield on AUM
   • Retail: SSSG, gross margin/sq ft
   Focus on gross margin drivers, working capital cycle, fixed cost leverage.

3. GROWTH DRIVERS & CATALYSTS
   • Near-term and structural triggers
   • Order book, expansion plans, new launches
   • Macro tailwinds: infra, policy, consumption
   • Management guidance credibility (past vs. actuals)

4. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING
   • Volume / margin / niche leadership?
   • Benchmark vs. peers on size, cost, ROCE, innovation
   • Moats: distribution, brand, cost, contracts, R&D
   • Competition: local / global / fragmented

5. VALUATION (sector-relevant metrics only)
   • EV/EBITDA, EV/tonne, Price/AUM, EV/bed — as appropriate
   • Compare to 5-year average and peer group
   • Is current price pricing in full growth or room for re-rating?

6. FINANCIAL SUMMARY (Q4FY25 / TTM only)
   • Revenue, EBITDA, PAT CAGR (3- or 5-year)
   • Margin trends, working capital days, inventory turns
   • Capex vs. FCF generation
   • ROCE vs. WACC (capital efficiency)
   • Balance sheet: net debt, D/E, interest coverage
   • CFO vs. accruals commentary

7. RISK ASSESSMENT
   • Sector risks + company-specific risks
   • Customer concentration, cyclicality
   • RM / currency volatility
   • Execution risk in expansion
   • Regulatory risk (NLEM, pollution, pricing caps)
   • Governance: pledging, opaque disclosures
   • Tech disruption / import substitution

8. INVESTMENT CHECKLIST (Yes/No + Comment)
   • Clear structural growth driver
   • Margin expansion tailwind
   • Capital efficiency (ROCE > Cost of Capital)
   • Clean balance sheet
   • Attractive valuation (below 5Y avg or peer discount)
   • Execution credibility (management has met past guidance)
   • No material governance red flag
   • Moat or entry barrier
   • ESG / regulatory compliance risk
   • Insider buying / promoter behaviour positive

9. BULL / BASE / BEAR SCENARIOS (Quantified)
   For each: revenue growth, EBITDA margin, PAT, target multiple, implied price
   Include IRR for a 2-year and 3-year horizon.
   Assign probability: Bull __% / Base __% / Bear __%

10. MANAGEMENT COMMENTARY
    • Tone from latest concall
    • Confidence triggers vs. red flags
    • Any guidance downgrades or tone shifts

11. CONCLUSION
    Frame as one of:
    • Secular compounder with earnings visibility
    • Turnaround with optionality
    • Cyclical play
    • Fully valued with limited upside

Use most recent data. Do not rely on FY24 unless it is the latest available.
```

## 17. Walk the Talk

Use when you have multiple years of concall transcripts and/or annual reports. Upload all of them together.

```
You are an expert equity analyst reviewing management execution track record.
I am uploading [COMPANY NAME]'s concall transcripts and annual reports
from FY20 to FY25.

Analyse the company's 'Walk the Talk' across this period.

Create a master tracking table with the following columns:
Year / Period | Management Guidance | Metric Type | Actual Outcome
             | Status (see below)   | Commentary

Status Key:
✅ Met (within 5% of guidance)
🟡 Partially Met (5–15% miss)
❌ Missed (>15% miss)
✅✅ Overachieved (exceeded by >10%)

Cover the following guidance types:
• Revenue growth targets
• EBITDA margin guidance
• Capex plans and commissioning timelines
• New product / segment launch timelines
• Export targets
• Debt repayment guidance
• Volume/capacity utilisation
• Any other specific quantitative guidance given

After the table, provide a SCORECARD:
• Overall delivery rate: ___% of guidance met or exceeded
• Areas of consistent underdelivery (3+ misses)
• Areas of consistent overdelivery
• Management credibility rating: High / Medium / Low
• Key question: Is management conservative or optimistic in its guidance style?

I will reward you if done well.
```
