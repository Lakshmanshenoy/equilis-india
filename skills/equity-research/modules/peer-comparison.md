# Peer comparison module

## How to identify peers
Do not use index constituency as the peer set. Use business model proximity:
1. Same primary revenue driver (e.g. commercial vehicles — not just "auto").
2. Similar scale (within 3x of subject company market cap, unless market is small).
3. Same regulatory environment (e.g. for NBFCs, compare only with NBFCs — not banks).
4. Preferably same listing exchange for data consistency (NSE preferred).

Aim for 4–5 peers. More dilutes the comparison; fewer is too thin.

## Premium / discount analysis
After the table, write a short paragraph addressing:
1. On which metrics does the subject company trade at a premium to peers?
2. Is this premium historically justified? (Check 3-year average P/E vs peers.)
3. On which metrics is it cheaper? Is that cheapness a value opportunity or a value trap?
4. What would need to be true for the premium/discount to close?

Never conclude "therefore it is cheap" or "therefore it is expensive" — present the
evidence and let the reader assess.
